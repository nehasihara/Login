What Is an IQ?

IQ (Intelligence Quotient) is a measure of a person's cognitive abilities or intelligence relative to others, typically assessed through standardized tests. It is designed to evaluate problem-solving, reasoning, and analytical skills.

History of Intelligence Testing

The history of intelligence testing began in the early 20th century with Alfred Binet, who developed the first standardized test to identify students needing educational support. Over time, it evolved with various models, including the Stanford-Binet and Wechsler tests, to measure human intelligence across different populations.
