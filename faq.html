Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ's)

An IQ test measures a range of cognitive abilities and provides a score that is intended to serve as a measure of an individual's intellectual abilities and potential.

An IQ test helps understand an individual's mental aptitude and ability to process and analyze information.

The results are used to assess an individual's intellectual capacity and can serve as a tool for identifying strengths and areas for development.

The score is based on standardized norms and compares an individual's performance to that of others within the same age group.

A high IQ score typically indicates exceptional intellectual ability in areas such as problem-solving and logical reasoning.

A lower score may indicate a need for educational support or a focus on skill development in specific areas.
